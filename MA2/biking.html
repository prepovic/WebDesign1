Good trails in the Seattle area

One of the most important parts of biking is finding the good trails to ride, here are a few of our recommended trails:

  • 520 Trail:

    This is a 2.7 mile long trail bordering the 520 bridge. It offers beautiful views of lake Washington, however, there are 2 slight negatives. First, the trail is right next to the highway, so there is quite a lot of noise pollution. Additionally, there is a 570 ft elevation gain, so be prepared to work hard.

  • Cross Kirkland Corridor:

    Cross Kirkland Corridor is a 5.2 mile long trail spanning all of Kirkland. It is about 6 feet wide and generally has very little elevation gain. Most of the trail is surrounded by trees and is a very clean place to enjoy the weekend.

  • Burke Gilman Trail:

    Bruke Gilman trail is an 18.4 mile long trail that is a personal favorite of mine. It warps around lake Washington and is mostly shaded. Along the trail there are many parks including Log Boom Park, Matthews Beach, and Magnuson Park.

How to bike safely

Biking is really fun, but it is also important to be aware that riding unprotected next to giant cars can pose a safety risk. Following the steps below recommended by the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration can really help, maybe even watch the video below!

  • Wear a helmet

    Helmets save more than 1,800 lives per year according to the CDC

  • Adjust your bike

    Adjusting your bike can save you from numb hands or toes, knee pain, lower back pain, shoulder discomfort

  • Check equipment before riding

    While checking equipment might be a 5 minute prodecure at home, it could save you many hours of rehab or repair later on

  • See and be seen

    Make sure to watch where you are going on your bike, even if it might not seem like it, you could really hurt someone if you went fast enough on a bike

  • Watch for road hazards

    A pothole or rouge tree root could lead to the end of your biking career, so watch out

  • Avoid riding at night

    Night is generally a bad time to be commuting, but if you have to, make sure there are lights on your bike and you wear bright clothes
